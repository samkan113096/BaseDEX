## WEEK 2 — Education

**Day 8 — Leverage Explainer**
```
📚 Leverage explained simply

Leverage = borrowed exposure

Example: 10× leverage on $100 margin
→ You control a $1,000 position
→ +10% price move = +$100 profit (100% return!)
→ -10% price move = -$100 loss (liquidation)

Key: Your risk is your margin, not your notional.

Start with 2–3× when learning. Increase only when comfortable.

Need help? Ask in the community chat 👇
```

**Day 9 — Funding Rates**
```
💡 What is a funding rate?

In perpetual futures, "funding" is a regular payment between longs and shorts.

Why it exists: To keep the perp price aligned with spot.

How it works:
• More longs than shorts → longs pay shorts (positive funding)
• More shorts than longs → shorts pay longs (negative funding)

Practical impact:
• Holding a long with +0.1%/8h funding = expensive
• Holding a short with -0.1%/8h funding = you earn money just by holding

Always check funding before entering! It's visible on the BaseDEX UI.
```

**Day 11 — Risk Management**
```
🔐 Risk management basics

The 1% rule:
Never risk more than 1% of your total capital on a single trade.

$1,000 portfolio → max $10 at risk
$10,000 portfolio → max $100 at risk
$100,000 portfolio → max $1,000 at risk

How to apply on BaseDEX:
1. Decide your max risk (e.g., $50)
2. Place stop loss at your exit level
3. Calculate position size accordingly
4. Set the stop-limit order BEFORE you enter

Protect capital first. Profit is secondary.
```

**Day 20 — Trading Tip**
```
💡 Trading tip of the day

Use the order book before placing trades.

Before entering a large position, look at:
1. The size of the spread (bid vs ask)
2. Depth at key price levels
3. Whether walls are real or spoofed

If the spread is wide or depth is thin → use a limit order
If the spread is tight and depth is deep → market order is fine

The order book tells you more than the chart. Use it.
```
